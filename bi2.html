Key Data Insights & Management Conclusions

Rainfall Impact (Strong Inverse Correlation): Dry years average 13.3M visitors (highest), Very Wet years average 12.1M visitors (lowest) – a 9.9% drop (1.2M fewer visitors) with increased rainfall.

Sunshine Impact (Weak Positive Correlation): Very Sunny years average 12.8M visitors, Cloudy years average 12.2M visitors – a 4.9% increase (0.6M more visitors) with more sunshine (far weaker than rainfall).

Notable Outlier: 2016 recorded 13.0M visitors (High category) despite 3026.8mm rainfall (Very Wet) – evidence of non-weather factors influencing park attendance.

Peak Visitation Year: 2011 (13.4M visitors) – coincided with 1487.2mm rainfall (Dry) and 1978.5h sunshine (Sunny) – the most favorable weather combination in the dataset.

Management Recommendation: Prioritize rainfall forecasts for safety alerts, indoor activity programming, and maintenance schedule adjustments. Future research should use monthly (not annual) data to account for seasonal weather variations and include temperature/air quality metrics.
